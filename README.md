## Project Overview

This project was completed during **Semester 3** of my **Master of Cyber Security** at **RMIT University** as part of the **Industry Awareness Project**.

Working as a **three-member team**, we conducted extensive research into the growing threat of phishing attacks against small-scale banks and investigated how Artificial Intelligence can improve cybersecurity by enabling faster threat detection, behavioural analysis, and automated defence mechanisms.

The project combines cybersecurity concepts, AI applications, banking security, real-world case studies, and industry best practices to propose practical recommendations for strengthening cyber resilience in financial institutions.

## Project Objectives

- Analyse cybersecurity challenges faced by small-scale banks.
- Study phishing and spear-phishing attack techniques.
- Examine how Artificial Intelligence is being used by attackers.
- Explore AI-driven defensive technologies.
- Evaluate real-world banking phishing incidents.
- Recommend cybersecurity improvements for financial institutions.

## Case Studies

The report analyses several real-world phishing incidents, including:

- National Australia Bank (NAB)
- Commonwealth Bank of Australia (CBA)
- Westpac
- Metro Bank
- Tesco Bank

These case studies were examined to understand attack techniques, organisational impacts, and defensive strategies.
